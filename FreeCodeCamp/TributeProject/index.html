Quentin Tarantino

Time Line

  • 1963 - Born in Knoxville on March 27, 1963, the only child of Connie McHugh and aspiring actor Tony Tarantino, who left the family before his son's bith.
  • 1966 - Return to Los Angeles
  • 1977 - Wrote one of his earliest works, a screenplay called Captain Peachfuzz and the Anchovy Bandit.
  • 1986 - Employed in his firt Hollywood job, working with Roger Avary as production assistans on Dolph Lundgren's exercise video.
  • 1992 - He wrote, directed, and acted in Reservoir Dogs as Mr. Brown. It was an immediate hit, with the film receiving a positive response from critics.
  • 1995 - Wrote, directed and acted in the black comedy crime film Pulp Fiction, maintaining the aestheticization of violence for which he is know, as well as his non-linear storylines. Tarantino received the Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay.
  • 2004 - Attended the 2004 Cannes Film Festival, where he served as President of the Jury.
  • 2009 - Tarantino's film Inglourious Basterds, released in 2009, is the story of a group of Jewish-American guerrilla soldiers in Nazi occupied France during WWII.
  • 2011 - Production began on Django Unchained, a film about the revenge of a former slave in the U.S South in 1858. The film stemmed from Tarantino's desire to produce a Spaghetti Western set in America's Deep South.
  • 2017 - It was reported that Tarantino's next project woul be a film about the Manson Family murders. In February 2018 it was announced that the film's title would be Once Upon a Time in Hollywood.
